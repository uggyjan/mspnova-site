Our story

Built inside a working help desk.

MSPNova came out of Techital, a managed services provider in Glendale, CA that's been running help desks for businesses across Southern California since 2001. We built Nova to solve our own dispatch problem. We still run our own help desk on it every day.

Why an MSP built its own software.

Techital has spent more than two decades supporting small and mid-sized businesses across Los Angeles and Orange County. That's two decades of mornings spent reading queues, chasing SLAs, and handing tickets to whichever engineer happened to be free.

We tried the obvious things first — workflow rules in our PSA, dispatcher checklists, ticket-routing tools. None of them survived contact with a busy Monday. So we started writing our own glue: a small service that read Autotask, ranked tickets by urgency, and posted a briefing to Teams.

That glue grew into Pulse. Pulse grew into triage. Triage grew into the platform we now call Nova. When peer MSPs in our network asked if they could buy it, we said yes — and Nova became a product. Every feature ships internally first.
