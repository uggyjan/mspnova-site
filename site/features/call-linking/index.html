The problem

An hour of phone work, ten minutes of ticket admin.

Calls come in faster than tickets do. A tech ends a 12-minute support call, then opens Autotask, searches for the company, opens five tickets to find the right one, copy-pastes a summary they wrote in their head, fixes the time entry, and moves on. Multiply by 30 calls a day across the team and you've burned an FTE on data entry that should be automatic.

Worse: half those calls don't get linked at all. The ticket reads "called user, resolved" with no transcript, no recording reference, nothing the next tech (or the QBR) can audit.

How matching works

From hangup to linked ticket in under a minute.

/ STEP 1

Sync from GoTo

Nova pulls call records from GoTo Connect every few minutes — direction, parties, duration, recording URL. No browser plugin, no softphone replacement.

/ STEP 2

Resolve company & contact

Caller-ID → Autotask contact lookup. Unknown numbers get logged with a "first time we've seen this caller" flag so dispatch can decide whether to add them.

/ STEP 3

Transcribe & summarize

Call audio is transcribed and an AI summary is written in plain English. Sentiment (positive / neutral / negative) is auto-tagged. Topic tags surface across the inbox.

/ STEP 4

Propose ticket matches

Nova ranks candidate tickets by content similarity, time proximity, company match, and active assignment. Top three appear next to the call with a "Link" button. Tech clicks once.

The matching signal

Four ranking signals, weighted together.

Every candidate ticket gets a score 0–100. Anything above 30% surfaces as a suggestion. The dispatcher and tech can override; overrides feed the learning loop.

CONTENT SIMILARITY

What was said vs. what's in the ticket

Embeddings compare the call summary against open ticket subjects, descriptions, and recent notes for the same company. Strong content match alone can push a ticket to 70%+.

TIME PROXIMITY

Tickets created or updated within the call window

A ticket logged 4 minutes after the call ended scores higher than one from yesterday. Combined with company match, time alone is often enough.

COMPANY MATCH

Caller-ID resolved to an Autotask company

Tickets for the same company always rank above unrelated ones. When caller-ID misses, Nova falls back to the assigned tech's open work for the time window.

ACTIVE ASSIGNMENT

Who's the tech actually working with

A ticket assigned to the tech on the call ranks above the same ticket sitting in someone else's queue. Stops cross-talk between dispatchers.

Live call state

The Status Board lights up when the phone rings.

GoTo Connect's webhooks push presence into Nova in real time. The dispatch wall shows who's on a call, with whom, and for how long — without anyone touching their status.

  • Inbound ringing → Status Board flips that tech to "on call (incoming)" with caller name + company
  • Outbound dial → "on call (outgoing)" with the dialed contact resolved against Autotask
  • Call duration ticker visible to dispatch — long calls (>20 min) flag for follow-up
  • Hung up → Nova waits 60s, then runs the linker and posts a Teams DM with the proposed ticket

When the proposed matches are wrong, search by ticket number, title, or company. The match becomes training data for next time.

FAQ

Common questions.

Do we need to switch our phone system?

No. GoTo Connect is the supported provider on day one. Microsoft Teams Phone is on the roadmap. If you're on RingCentral, Zoom Phone, or Dialpad — tell us; we add providers based on customer ask.

What about HIPAA / sensitive call content?

Recordings stay in GoTo. Nova stores the transcript and summary; both can be purged per-call or retention-capped per tenant. Anthropic's enterprise terms (zero retention) cover the AI summarization.

How accurate is the auto-matching?

Roughly 80% of inbound calls land on the correct ticket as the top suggestion at typical MSPs we've measured. Time-proximity-only matches are flagged so the tech knows the system isn't certain.

Can we hide certain call types from the inbox?

Yes. Per-extension and per-line filters — internal calls, voicemail-only, calls under 30 seconds. Configured in Admin → Phone Integration.

What if the caller isn't in Autotask?

Nova logs the call with the caller-ID and a "create contact" prompt. The dispatcher can add them to a company, leave the call as orphaned, or hide it (e.g., a personal call).

Does it consume Anthropic tokens for every call?

Yes — transcript + summary use your Anthropic key. Typical 10-minute call is < $0.05. Calls under 30 seconds skip summarization by default.
