Composite scoring

Five factors. One score. No gut calls.

SLA risk

Time-to-breach weighted exponentially as the deadline closes. A ticket with 30 min on the clock outranks one with 4 hours, even if it came in later.

Client tier

Tier 1 clients (managed services contract, agreed response times) get a multiplier. Tier 3 (T&M, ad hoc) sits behind. Tunable per contract.

Age

Tickets older than 24 hours that aren't waiting on the client get an aging boost — small at first, growing daily.

Skill match

Each ticket carries a primary skill tag (M365, networking, on-site, etc.). The Pull Ticket flow scores against the requesting tech's skill profile.

Scarcity

If only one tech can handle a ticket type today (sole on-call, only certified person), Nova surfaces it before broader-skill tickets queue up.

Override

Dispatcher can pin any ticket to the top of the queue — VIP issue, internal escalation, owner ask. Pinned tickets show a manual flag.

/ Pull Ticket

Techs ask for work. Nova picks one.

Engineers don't browse a queue and pick what looks easy. They click "Pull next ticket." Nova considers their skills, current load, and the queue's composite scores, and assigns the single best fit. They can decline (with a reason) — that signal trains the model too.

  • Anti-cherry-picking by design — no list browsing
  • Decline w/ reason: "out of skill," "context mismatch," "need on-site"
  • Rebalance algorithm prevents one tech getting all the easy tickets
  • Optional manual mode for senior techs / leads
/ Holding lanes

Waiting on the client doesn't mean lost.

Tickets in "waiting on customer" pause SLA but stay tracked. Nova surfaces them when the client replies — or when they've been silent past the chase threshold. No more tickets falling into the void of someone else's inbox.

  • Pause SLA when status flips to waiting
  • Auto-resurface when client replies (Outlook integration)
  • Stale-chase alert at 3, 7, 14 days (configurable)
  • Auto-close suggestions for clearly resolved tickets
