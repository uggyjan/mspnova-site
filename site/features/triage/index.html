How it works

One pass. Reasoning attached. Dispatcher in the loop.

01 / WATCH

Adaptive polling

Nova checks Autotask every 30 seconds during business hours, every 5 minutes off-hours, every 15 minutes overnight. New tickets get picked up before your dispatcher refreshes.

02 / CLASSIFY

7 dimensions, <30s

Subject, body, contact context, and recent history get read in one pass. Out comes priority, issue type, queue, category, sub-category, work type, and source — with reasoning for each call.

03 / DECIDE

Dispatcher reviews

Suggestions land in a clean review list with the AI's reasoning visible. One click to accept, one click to override. Every override flows back as a training signal for the next pass.

/ Reasoning, not black-box

Every classification shows its work.

Nothing about Nova's calls is opaque. Each ticket card surfaces the reasoning the AI used — what signals in the subject, body, or contact history led to the priority, the queue, the category. Your dispatcher reads the reasoning, agrees or disagrees, and clicks once. The decision and the override (if any) are logged.

  • Plain-language reasoning on every field — priority, queue, category, work type
  • Click any field to override; reasoning travels with the override into history
  • Audit log: who accepted, who overrode, when, why
  • The dispatcher is always source of truth — Nova learns from them
/ Tunable per queue

Auto-route routine work. Hand-review what matters.

High-volume routine queues — password resets, license requests, printer reboots — can auto-route at high confidence. Sensitive queues (security incidents, P1s, VIP clients) stay 100% dispatcher-reviewed. You set the policy per queue and per client tier from the admin panel.

  • Auto-route policy per queue, per client tier
  • P1 and security tickets always land in dispatch review
  • Daily classification report posted to Teams
  • Pause AI for any queue from the admin panel — Nova falls back to manual
What changes

Less sorting. More dispatching.

Dispatchers spend most of a Monday morning sorting overnight tickets — reading subjects, guessing priority, picking the right queue. Nova does that work the moment a ticket hits Autotask. Your dispatcher opens their laptop and the queue is already organized. The job becomes deciding, not sorting.

We built this for ourselves first. At our parent MSP, triage went from a morning ritual to a few minutes of confirmations.
